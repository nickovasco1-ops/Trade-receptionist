## Design Context

### Users
UK sole-trader tradespeople aged 28–55: plumbers, electricians, HVAC engineers, builders, gas engineers. They drive vans, work with their hands, and check their phones between jobs — often in bright daylight on a phone screen. They are skeptical of tech, value plain language and real outcomes, and lose £3k–£15k/year in missed calls. Mobile-first. Time-poor. No patience for filler.

Secondary audience: small trade businesses (2–10 employees) with the same pain at greater scale.

### Brand Personality
**Authoritative. Precise. Built to last.**

Three words: *industrial*, *luminescent*, *purposeful*.

The visual metaphor is a master craftsman's workshop — warm industrial lighting on dark cast iron, blueprint-on-workbench precision, high-vis orange against deep navy. The product is a precision tool, not a startup app. Never flashy, never playful, never AI-tropey.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Industrial Luminescence"** — dark navy grounds, orange-to-amber accent, blueprint grid texture, glassmorphism panels that glow rather than float. Space Grotesk for display, Manrope for body. Reference quality: audia.framer.website — match scroll-driven reveals and ambient float but in this palette and with heavier, more industrial weight.

Anti-references: purple gradient SaaS, thin Helvetica corporate, robotic AI imagery, cartoon UI, light-grey enterprise apps.

Theme: **dark** — users read in vans, on rooftops, under sinks. Dark mode reduces eye strain in these conditions and reinforces the industrial precision feel.

### Design Principles
1. **Outcome over feature** — every headline, label, and button names what the user gains, not what the system does.
2. **Mechanical motion** — animations feel like precision machinery starting up. Minimum 300ms transforms. Never bouncy or springy.
3. **Tonal elevation, never lines** — sections and cards separate via background tonal shift and glassmorphism, not borders or dividers.
4. **One gradient keyword maximum** — at most one italic orange-gradient word per headline. Never the whole heading.
5. **Dashboard restraint** — marketing surfaces are editorial and animated. Dashboard surfaces are calm, dense, functional. Float animations and theatrical typography stay on marketing only.
